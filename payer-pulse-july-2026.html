Medicaid work requirements: Jan 1, 2027 deadline stands while 26 states sue

CMS's interim final rule (CMS-2454-IFC) implements OBBBA's 80-hours/month community-engagement requirement for expansion adults, projecting ~2.3M enrollment losses in FY2027; comments close and the rule takes effect Jul 31, 2026. On Jun 29, 26 states sued, arguing CMS unlawfully narrowed the "medically frail" exemption — timelines are now uncertain, but states and MCOs must keep building.

Why it matters: Verification, exemption, and churn-communication workflows touch eligibility config (B1), eligibility-to-claims integration (C1), and member outreach (X1) in every expansion state.
The play → Medicaid MCO COO/CIO conversation: "your Jan 1 build can't wait for the courts — here's how plans are de-risking eligibility config now."

2027 ACA filings: 14% median proposed increase; 20 insurers ask for 20%+

KFF's Jul 8 analysis of preliminary 2027 marketplace filings shows a second straight year of double-digit requests, driven by ~10% medical/pharmacy trend, enhanced-subsidy expiration, and the 2027 NBPP's market rule changes (standardized-plan requirement eliminated, user fees cut to 1.9%). Why it matters: Every exchange issuer is redesigning and repricing its 2027 portfolio under new NBPP rules — peak season for plan configuration, filing support, and SBC regeneration.
The play → VP Product at exchange issuers: benchmark their 2027 portfolio rework timeline against filing/AEP dates; B1 compresses it.

Stars recalculation saga: Clover wins, CMS reruns everyone, Elevance sues over $115M

After a Georgia court ruled CMS improperly included 20 measures in 2026 Star Ratings, CMS raised Clover's flagship PPO to 4.5 stars, then said in a Jun 17 memo it would voluntarily recalculate 2027 quality-bonus ratings for certain other contracts. On Jul 1, Elevance sued in S.D. Georgia claiming Clover got a more favorable remedy — ~$115M in 2027 bonus payments across five MA contracts at stake — and SCAN and Alignment have since filed their own suits. KFF pegs 2026 MA bonuses at $13B+.

Why it matters: Stars and risk-adjustment data integrity are now litigation-grade. Plans need audit-defensible quality data pipelines (I1) and clean benefits-to-claims config feeding the measures (C1).
The play → MA quality/analytics leaders at tracked plans: "if $115M can turn on measure math, what does your ratings data lineage look like?"

CMS stands up Office of Health Technology and Products to drive interoperability

Announced Jun 9 under Deputy Administrator Amy Gleason, OHTP centralizes CMS strategy for AI, digital products, provider directories, and claims-system modernization, including a Division of Data and Interoperability Platforms. It's the enforcement muscle behind the payer API agenda — CMS-0057 prior-auth/interop APIs are due Jan 1, 2027.

Why it matters: Directory accuracy (P1), claims/prior-auth data exchange (C1), and member-facing transparency (X1) all sit on OHTP's runway — federal attention means budgets follow.
